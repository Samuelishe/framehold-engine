# PRIVACY_AND_OPERATOR_RESPONSIBILITIES

## Distinctions

Framehold Engine documentation distinguishes:

1. Framehold Engine software project.
2. A specific deployed Framehold Engine instance.
3. The operator of that deployed instance.
4. Portfolio Owners.
5. Public Visitors.

The open-source license governs software use and distribution. It is not a privacy policy or Terms of Service for every deployed instance.

## Operator responsibilities

Each instance operator remains responsible for:

- privacy information;
- legal basis for personal-data processing;
- retention periods;
- email-delivery provider;
- VPS and storage providers;
- backup provider;
- abuse contact;
- applicable jurisdiction;
- account suspension;
- content complaints;
- copyright complaints;
- security incident response;
- data-erasure handling.
- optional analytics or external service disclosure when explicitly configured.

## Planned configurable pages

Framehold Engine should eventually provide configurable or template pages for:

- Privacy Policy;
- Terms of Service;
- Open-source license information;
- Third-party notices;
- Contact;
- Copyright or content complaint information.

This task does not draft jurisdiction-specific legal documents.

## Non-claims

Do not claim that:

- AGPL removes all operator responsibilities;
- an as-is disclaimer replaces a privacy policy;
- self-hosting automatically guarantees privacy compliance;
- account deletion automatically satisfies all laws.
- built-in themes secretly activate analytics, telemetry or external tracking.
